Analyzing UNESCO's Accessibility Narratives

With this work we will be analyzing a selection of UNESCO documents to understand how their focus on accessibility in heritage has changed over time.

The goal is to identify shifts in specific aspects of accessibility depending on the heritage type and period.

The project will take into account different types of accessibility: physical, intellectual, economic, social, and cultural and different types of heritage (tangible and intangible, cultural, natural, and digital).

Get Started

Corpus selection

Our source of reference for the selection of the documentation has been uniquely the Unesco Digital Library .

We have been performing a specific research on the Digital Library based on the following criteria:

  • Main Topic: (All Words) “Accessibility”
  • Year of publication: from 2020 to 2023
  • Source: Unesco
  • Official language: English

Our search (performed in June 2023) returned a list of 74 documents as result, then in September it arrived to 77.

There are different kinds of documents in the list, most of them classified as programme and meeting documents are messages from the Director-General of Unesco, whitepaper, information papers, report and draft report on specific activities,then we have books, periodical issues and articles.

Our Research Questions

  • How has UNESCO's focus on accessibility in heritage changed over time, and what are the specific areas of accessibility and heritage that they tend to focus on?

  • What types of accessibility (physical, intellectual, economic, social, cultural) are focused on in UNESCO documents?

  • What types of heritage (tangible and intangible, cultural, natural, and digital) are focused on in UNESCO documents?

  • How does the focus of UNESCO’s accessibility efforts vary across different types of heritage (tangible/intangible, cultural/natural/digital)?

  • How has UNESCO's focus on accessibility in heritage changed over time and specifically after pandemic?

Our Methodology

  • Collect a corpus of UNESCO documents related to heritage and accessibility.

  • Preprocess the text data by removing stop words, stemming or lemmatizing, and tokenizing the documents.

  • Perform topic modeling on the corpus using a tool such as Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) to identify the key topics and themes in the documents. Frequencies and co-occurrence etc. (things from TRAM)

  • Analyze the topics and themes to identify the specific areas of accessibility and heritage that are being focused on in the documents.

  • Conduct a longitudinal analysis to track changes in focus over time by dividing the corpus into different time periods and analyzing the topics and themes in each period.

  • Use visualization techniques such as word clouds and network graphs to present the findings in an accessible and informative manner.

The Process

This project focuses on text processing and analysis, providing functionalities such as topic modeling, sentiment analysis, and text cleaning. The detailed documentation below provides insights into the modules used, functions defined, and the methodologies implemented.

1. Imports and setup

In this section, the script prepares the environment for text processing and analysis.

2. Directory definitions

In this section, the script specifies the directories where the text documents are located and where the processed documents will be saved.

3. Text cleaning functions

Text cleaning is a crucial step in any text processing or analysis task. In this section, two functions are defined to perform text cleaning.

4. Processing the corpus

In this section, the script reads documents from the source directory, cleans them, and then saves the cleaned documents to the output directory.

5. Creating a dictionary and corpus for topic modeling

Topic modeling requires two main structures: a dictionary and a corpus.

6. Topic modeling

Topic modeling is a type of statistical model used for discovering abstract topics in a collection of documents. In this script, Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is employed as the topic modeling technique.

7. Assigning dominant topics to documents

After discovering the topics in the corpus, it's helpful to know which topic is most prominent in each document.

8. Sentiment analysis

Sentiment analysis is the process of determining the emotional tone or sentiment behind a piece of text. In this section, the script calculates the sentiment for each document and categorizes it as Positive, Neutral, or Negative.
